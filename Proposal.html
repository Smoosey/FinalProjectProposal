Overview

My final project will be to design and develop a personal portfolio website to showcase my professional and personal projects. The design of the website will be clean, modern, and visually appealing, with a focus on easy navigation and user experience. The website will consist of approximately six pages, including a home page, about me page, resume page, project page, blog page, and contact page.

Pages

  • Home: featuring a welcome message, a brief introduction of myself, and links to the other pages.
  • About Me: providing more detailed information about myself, including my education, skills, and experience.
  • Resume: displaying my professional experience, education, and skills in an easy-to-read format.
  • Projects: showcasing some of my professional and personal projects, including descriptions and screenshots.
  • Blog: featuring some of my blog posts on web development and technology.
  • Contact: providing a contact form for visitors to send me messages or inquiries.

Styling

The website will use a color scheme of greens and grays, with some accents of orange. The fonts will be clean and easy to read, such as Arial or Helvetica. The layout will be responsive, adapting to different screen sizes, and will have a clean, modern feel. Navigation will be easy to use and consistent throughout the website.

Functionality

JavaScript will be used to provide some interactivity and functionality to the website. For example, the navigation bar will collapse on smaller screens and expand on larger screens. The contact form will use JavaScript to validate user input before submitting the form.

Repository Organization

The repository will be organized into several folders, including one for HTML, one for CSS, and one for JavaScript. There will also be separate folders for images and other media files. Files will be named and organized logically, with descriptive names and clear directory structures.

Inspiration

The primary source of inspiration for the portfolio design is minimalist and modern websites, such as those of design agencies and tech companies. I also drew inspiration from websites that focus on web development and technology, such as CSS-Tricks and Smashing Magazine.
